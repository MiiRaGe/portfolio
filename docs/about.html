About me

A bit of history…

I grew up in a small town in the North East of France, close to Strasbourg. I studied Software Engineering in Paris and Human-Computer-Interaction in Karlkrona (yep, that's the start of my story with Sweden!).

I’ve worked in Nice, London and Stockholm, more recently at H&M. Before that, I was at Travel company Thomas Cook, consultancy System Concepts, and technology provider Amadeus. You can learn more in my resume.

What am I like?

Honestly, I don't like talking about myself all that much (wow, that's original!). Writing out some blurb about all my supposed qualities makes me suuuuper uncomfortable. How would you even know if it's true?

I've been following a course about the science of well-being and one assignment was to fill in this quiz to find out your character strengths... So I thought, hey, that's probably a more objective view! So voilà, here they are, my top strengths:

  • Humility
  • Judgement
  • Perspective
  • Kindness
  • Appreciation of beauty and elegance

My colleagues usually describe me as kind, honest, wise, and a great source for feedback.

At work, I care about...

Making stuff that bring value to people's life, and work for all people.

My background is in science, so of course I'm pretty obsessed about research and data, and making sure we have a good understanding of the problem we're trying to solve.

I also care deeply about accessibility. At first, just because it obviously is the right thing to do, and because of a few people close to me. But I became even more driven when I started running accessibility testing, as I came to realise how much I still had to learn, and started to understand the true extent of the misery some careless design decisions can unleash.
