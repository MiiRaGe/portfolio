Re-designing a steel inspection software

A technology provider for the steel industry - 2015

The challenge

Improve the usability and aesthetics of a software used to inspect steel rolls.

What I did

I worked with two other consultants who were respectively in charge of the research and visual design, while I was in charge of UX design and interaction design.

In-context research and competitor review

The first step was to familiarise myself with the technical documentation and meet with the key stakeholders, while my colleague went on site to observe users of the product we were re-designing and users of its competitor. This allowed us to:

  • gauge which elements were the most important to allow operators to make decisions.
  • understand what kind of challenges users faced due to the environment in which they work.
  • identify strengths and weaknesses in the market to prioritise changes.

Ideation workshop

I conducted an ideation workshop with the key stakeholders. This allowed us to make sure everyone felt involved in the design process (and excited about it!). By the end, we had collaboratively outlined the key journeys, agreed on how information should be prioritised, and produced paper sketches illustrating concurrent ideas.

Wireframes

I created a series of wireframes that allowed to visualise the new flow and layout of the pages, then iterated a couple of times to address any concerns that stakeholder might have had regarding technical limitations.

High fidelity mockups and style guide

Our visual designer created high-fidelity visualisations based on the wireframes, as well as a new set of icons for the interface.

We then worked together on a document summing up how to implement the new layout and explaining the interactions.

Results

  • The number of steps required to complete a roll inspection have been reduced by focusing on what operators really need to make decisions.
  • Actionnable elements are bigger so they can be activated even when wearing gloves.
