Increasing sign ups

H&M - 2020

The challenge

15% of H&M online customers are not members of the loyalty program. They are missing out on cheaper, more personalised shopping... And members have a higher life time value for H&M.

So, how do we encourage more customers to sign up?

What I did

I was the Product Designer in a new Product Team in charge of increasing the number of sign ups.

Initial Research

The team was bombarded with lots of requests to add more features, or put the sign up button in more places. But there was little data to back up those requests.

So, I started by gathering insights:

  • conducting user testing (both in-person and remotely), that I invited the team to watch.
  • asking feedback from market experts and store staff (who see people struggling to sign up at the till).
  • looking at Clicktale and Google Analytics.
  • checking results from previous A/B tests

With this information, the product owner and I established our priority for the upcoming sprints, based on effort and impact:

  • fix the basics in the sign up form (technical flaws and usability issues)
  • promote sign up more, but in the right context (for instance, at checkout)
  • adapt to specific markets

I built a quick showcase of our research for our Sprint Demo. This helped stakeholders to understand our strategy and accept that we would not be focusing on some of the features they were keen on.

Design - Fix the basics

Different sign up forms were used at different point of the journey, which was hard to maintain. I provided consistent design for all of them, aligned with our new Design System.

Another small fix to make was around the the date of birth (a mandatory field) that was difficult to input on mobile (especially on Android). This is a common issue, so I based my solution on existing research (for instance: Research by gov.uk) and guidelines (for instance: Material Design Date pickers)

Ideation workshop

A key opportunity we identified was to promote joining in the checkout. It is where it makes the most sense to users: they get immediate benefits from it (like free shipping and discounts).

The checkout is an area many teams have a stake in. So, I invited business experts, designers, product owners and analysts to a workshop to generate ideas together.

However, the schedule had been a little bit too tight to make the most out of that positive energy. I had added more time due to the workshop being fully remote (we had just started working from home due to Covid-19). But that was not enough: a good learning for me!

Design & test: Support international growth

We set out to provide different, locally-adapted ways to sign up. This has not been launched yet so I will show some of the process, but details are obscured.

Results

As a direct result of the changes implemented on mobile:

  • The average time it takes to join the loyalty program was reduced by 25s.
  • Drop outs in the sign up form decreased by 10%.

The first A/B tests around promoting sign up in the checkout resulted in increased sign ups. Some other ideas are still being tested.

The local sign up has not launched yet.
