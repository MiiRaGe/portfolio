Creating a financial support tool

Money Advice Service and Department of Work and Pensions

The challenge

The Money Advice Service was commissioned by the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) to build a website offering financial support to people on Universal Credit. The original prototype didn't meet users' needs at all, so they decided to start from scratch.

My role

As a UX consultant at System Concepts, I:

  • Assisted the lead researcher (note-taking, analysis, communicating the findings).
  • Facilitated workshops with stakeholders to define user needs, outline personas and map key user journeys.
  • Produced sketches, wireframes and a high-fidelity responsive prototype in Axure.

I worked alongside a researcher from the DWP.

The work

Interviews

Given the limited time that was allocated to the discovery phase, the best way to gain a better understanding of our target audience was to go meet them directly at Job Centres and conduct interviews. We focused on uncovering how our users managed their money and what their pain points were.

Workshops

I held a series of workshops to collectively digest the findings from the interviews and define the user needs and personas. All the key stakeholders (who came from different organisations) were involved.

Personas, User Needs & User Journeys

I finalised the prioritised list of user needs, personas and user journeys that were initially discussed during the workhops. Having these materials was useful later on to inform the design and the content creation.

Low-fidelity prototype

Based on the insights from the discovery phase, I produced sketches, and then low-fidelity wireframes in Axure. These were regularly shared with stakeholders, taken out for guerrilla testing and updated. For the first iterations we focused on the mobile versions of the product, before moving on to other screen sizes.

High-fidelity prototype

When we were satisfied that the product was going in the right direction, I created a higher fidelity fully responsive prototype to allow us to test the interaction mechanisms during lab testing sessions with users. For this prototype, I worked with a visual designer and a copywriter to refine the visual design and content.

Key achievements

  • We designed a MVP that answered real user needs while still meeting key expectations from the people who commissioned the tool.
