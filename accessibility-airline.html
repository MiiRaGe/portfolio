Improving the accessibility of airline websites

Amadeus and various airlines - 2013/2016

The challenge

The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) issued a rule requiring Airlines' websites to comply to level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.0 (WCAG v2.0) by the end of 2015. I have worked to achieve this goal with Amadeus on the e-Retail booking system, which is used by over a hundred airlines, as well as directly with various airlines such as Japan Airlines, Avianca or China Southern Airlines.

The work

Audits

I have conducted multiple audits against the WCAG 2.0 guidelines. When conducting the audits, I examine the pages with:

  • common assistive technologies (such as the screen reader JAWS)
  • specific accessibility settings (like inverted colors)
  • tools like the Web Developper browser extension

This allows to spot problems. I then dive into the code of the pages to pin-point what causes the issues and how to fix them.

Testing

I have conducted accessibility testing with users who have various accessibility needs, to help ensure the sites were truly accessible.

Training

I have delivered short training sessions to teams at Amadeus to help them understand why accessibility matters and how they could personally contribute to improve the accessibility of pages.

Key achievements

  • Big accessibility issues have been addressed throughout the booking flow: there are no more problems that would completely stop one category of users from accessing a page (for instance, all elements can be accessed with a keyboard).
  • All the work done to improve accessibility has also been used to improve the general usability and look and feel of the pages, as well as to clean up the code.
